About Me

Kaushal Ramalingam

Hello! My name is Kaushal Ramalingam. I am a 14 year old student in 9th grade studying at Dublin High School. In the future, I plan on working in a field that combines the computer science field with the political line, possibly as a policy analyst or an IT analyst under the government. I plan on receiving my bachelor's degree in computer science then receiving a master's degree in computer engineering. I hope to attain early experience in the workforce by doing an internship in a related area in high school or in my undergraduate or graduate years. I wish to work in such a field because I am extremely interested in working with software and technology, and I want to be able to contribute these skills to the government and the country.

I am extremely passionate in speech and debate, competing with my middle school as well as participating on the Dublin High team, becoming captain of the LD division this year. I have placed and received speaker awards at tournaments across the nation, most notably reaching semifinals at the national tournament in Florida last year. Moreover, I love to play tennis in my free time. I play competitively, taking part at tournaments across the state, and I am looking to play for the Dublin High boys tennis team in the spring. In addition, I play an active role in my community through the National Junior Honor Society. In eighth grade, I was elected president of NJHS and amassed more than 70 hours of community service, from volunteering at the local homeless shelter to going door to door raising awareness about gun violence.

I would describe myself as a motivated student, striving to to achieve my best in the activities I participate in. As a freshman whose hopes are to work in the engineering field, I am setting my sights on getting into the Engineering and Design Academy at Dublin High, as well as taking advantage of opportunities relating to the STEM field. I have completed a few projects in this area, and I am looking to do more. You can learn more about this in the Projects page. Thanks for reading. You can read more about me in the Resume tab!

Feel free to contact me on the Contact Me page!
